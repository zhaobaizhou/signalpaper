Decision Archive is a personal knowledge tool designed around one insight: decisions are more valuable than notes.

Notes capture what you observed. Decisions capture what you *chose*, and more importantly, *why* you chose it.

## The problem it solves

Most note-taking systems optimize for capture. You write things down so you don't forget them. But over time, the system becomes a graveyard of half-remembered observations.

Decision Archive is different. It only stores entries that answer: "Given what I knew at the time, this is what I decided and why."

## Format

Each decision entry includes:

- **Context**: What situation prompted this decision?
- **Options considered**: What alternatives did you evaluate?
- **Choice**: What did you decide?
- **Reasoning**: Why this option over the others?
- **Expected outcome**: What did you expect to happen?
- **Actual outcome**: (filled in later) What actually happened?

## Status

Currently in active use. The core format is stable. Building a simple UI for browsing and reviewing past decisions.
