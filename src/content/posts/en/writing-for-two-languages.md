Bilingual publishing is not just translation. It is a structure decision.

SignalPaper keeps English content at the root and Chinese content under `/zh`. Posts and projects live in parallel content folders, so each language can have its own title, summary, tags, and body.

## Why separate content folders

Separate folders make the editorial model explicit. You can translate a post one-to-one, adapt it for another audience, or publish only one language when needed.

This is easier to maintain than hiding language variants inside a single file.

## Metadata matters

Each language should have its own description, title, Open Graph text, and page content. Search engines and AI systems read those signals separately.

## Keep the switch simple

The language switcher should be visible but quiet. It is a navigation control, not the center of the page.

Good bilingual publishing is not about showing that two languages exist. It is about making both versions feel native.
