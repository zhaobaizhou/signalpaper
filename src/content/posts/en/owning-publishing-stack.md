Platforms are useful for distribution. They are not a stable home for long-term work.

Owning your publishing stack means your URLs, archive, metadata, and presentation are not entirely dependent on another product's incentives.

## What ownership gives you

You control the structure of the archive. You decide how posts relate to projects. You can keep old work available, update metadata, and make the site readable without chasing feed mechanics.

## What ownership does not solve

It does not guarantee readers. It does not replace distribution. You still need to share work where people already spend time.

The practical approach is simple: publish canonical work on your own site, then distribute links elsewhere.

## Why a static site works well

A static Astro site keeps the maintenance surface small. Markdown content, typed content collections, build-time search, and static deployment are enough for many author sites.
