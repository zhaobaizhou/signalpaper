This is the first essay on a new site. That's a strange position to be in.

There's no archive yet. No proof of consistency. No track record. Just this one piece, and whatever impression it makes.

That constraint is actually clarifying. When you have nothing to fall back on, you have to say something real.

## What "first" means

A first post isn't really about introducing yourself. Introductions are boring. They're a genre of avoidance—you talk about what you're *going to* write instead of writing it.

A first post is better used as a demonstration. Pick a genuine question you've been thinking about and work through it in public. Not a hot take. Not a prediction. Just honest reasoning.

The reader doesn't need to know your background. They need to see your mind.

## The archive trap

Most sites get better as they age. More posts, more signal, more context. But they also get harder to navigate. The most important work gets buried.

SignalPaper is built around the opposite problem: what do you show when the archive is small?

The answer is: make the important work visible early. Use `featured: true` to surface the essays you stand behind. Use `summary` to give the reader one more line of context before they click.

Don't hide your best thinking behind chronology.
