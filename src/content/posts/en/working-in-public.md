There's a version of working in public that's actually just performing.

It looks like updates. Progress reports. "Day 47 of building in public." Thread after thread of incremental movement that serves the feed more than it serves the work.

I'm not interested in that version.

## What working in public actually requires

Working in public requires having something worth showing. Not a highlight reel—a real artifact. An essay. A decision. A tool someone can use. Something with enough weight that it justifies the space it takes up.

The log is not the work. The log is commentary on the work. It might be useful, but it's easy to confuse the two and optimize for the commentary instead.

## Showing the thinking, not just the doing

The better version of working in public is externalizing *how* you're reasoning, not just *what* you're doing.

Not: "I shipped feature X today."

But: "I chose to build X instead of Y because I think Z, and here's what I'm still not sure about."

That second version teaches something. It leaves a record worth returning to. It builds trust with readers who care about reasoning, not just output.

## The practical question

Before sharing an update, ask: *Is this a real artifact or just a signal that I'm still moving?*

If it's the latter, it might be better kept in a private log.
