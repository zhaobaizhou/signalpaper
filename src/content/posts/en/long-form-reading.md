Long-form reading works best when the interface is helpful without becoming the main event. The page should give readers orientation, then get out of the way.

SignalPaper keeps the article surface simple: clear typography, visible metadata, a restrained table of contents, reading progress, and related writings after the article.

## Reading progress

The reading progress bar is intentionally small. It answers one question: how far through the article am I? It does not need a label, a card, or a dashboard.

## Table of contents

The table of contents is useful on long essays and documentation pages. It should help readers jump between sections without competing with the prose.

## Related writings

Related writings are based on shared tags first, then recent posts. This gives the archive a sense of continuity without requiring authors to manually curate every connection.

## The default rule

The article page should feel like a place for reading, not a control panel. Add features only when they support attention.
