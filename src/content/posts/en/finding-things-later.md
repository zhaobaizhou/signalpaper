Most homepage visits are temporary. Archives are where a site becomes useful.

SignalPaper gives readers several ways back into older material: tag pages, paginated archives, related writings, RSS, and full-text search powered by Pagefind.

## Tags create paths

Tags should be broad enough to connect multiple pieces, but specific enough to mean something. A tag with one post is a label. A tag with a pattern is navigation.

## Search catches intent

Search is useful when a reader remembers a phrase, title, or concept but not the route. Because SignalPaper is static, Pagefind keeps this fast without requiring a server.

## Archives need rhythm

Pagination is not glamorous, but it matters. A reader should be able to move through older work without feeling lost.

Good findability turns a collection of posts into a durable site.
